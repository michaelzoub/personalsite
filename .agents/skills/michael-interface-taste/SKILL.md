# Michael Interface Taste

Design for an interesting thinker who does not need to announce that he is a serious builder. Let the work imply competence. Keep the page quiet enough for peculiar ideas and personal details to register without becoming self-conscious.

## Direction

- Combine pale blue with quiet editorial restraint.
- Stay light-only. Use warm white and near-white blue surfaces rather than pure white everywhere.
- Prefer precise typography, generous negative space, and low-contrast depth.
- Avoid portfolio theater, founder posturing, grand claims, decorative gradients, loud glass, and conspicuous cleverness.
- Let writing, current fascinations, Montréal, music, and long-horizon projects supply personality.

## Composition

- Keep the narrative or changing project text on the left and the work on the right at desktop widths.
- Use equal-size project cards so the sequence feels deliberate.
- Give each card character through its internal art direction, not a different outer size.
- Make cards feel like objects approaching the viewer during vertical scroll: modest scale, vertical travel, and at most a few degrees of perspective.
- Keep one card visually dominant at a time.
- Collapse to a calm single-column sequence on mobile; do not preserve theatrical 3D motion at the cost of reading.

## Cards

- Use one shared silhouette, radius, header rhythm, media proportion, and metadata structure.
- Vary media treatment by project: paper, terminal, pale instrument panel, editorial image, or typographic field note.
- Use layered low-opacity shadows and pure neutral image outlines.
- Keep descriptions concise and specific. Do not add filler labels or generic promotional copy.
- Prefer one quiet arrow or action cue over buttons inside every card.

## Personal signal

- Lead with curiosity, not credentials.
- Describe work through the kinds of systems and questions being explored.
- Use writing as the strongest personal surface.
- Mention the future autonomous farm as a small horizon, not a manifesto: self-sufficiency, physical work, and technology receding into the background.
- When representing the farm visually, build a miniature functional instrument with restrained telemetry rather than a literal illustration.

## Motion and sound

- Keep scroll depth smooth, continuous, and restrained. The effect should be felt before it is noticed.
- Use interruptible transforms and opacity. Avoid bounce.
- Keep user-triggered transitions under 300ms; longer motion is allowed only for slow ambient details or scroll-linked movement.
- Honor reduced motion by removing scroll transforms while preserving hierarchy.
- Use a short, low-volume muted keyboard-switch sound when a new card settles or the user takes a deliberate action.
- Never produce unsolicited ambient audio. Initialize sound on the visitor's first wheel, pointer, touch, or keyboard action because browsers require interaction.

## Review questions

Before finishing, ask:

1. Does the interface imply taste and competence without claiming either?
2. Does every card share a system while retaining its own internal character?
3. Is pale blue acting as atmosphere rather than decoration?
4. Is the personal detail specific, understated, and true?
5. Does motion clarify focus without turning the portfolio into a demo reel?
6. Would removing any label, shadow, animation, or sentence make the page stronger?
